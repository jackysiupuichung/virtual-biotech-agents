System architecture · automatic target identification & validation

An agentic AI system that identifies, researches, and validates therapeutic targets — and can prove every step.

A chief-of-staff agent decomposes a target question, routes it across specialist divisions that call expert tools against real biomedical databases, validates the signal in silico and against experimental & clinical evidence, audits itself, and synthesizes a graded GO / NO-GO call — with every claim recorded as a verifiable, replayable reasoning chain.

Layer 1 · agentic loop

It reasons like a discovery team.

A chief-of-staff agent frames the question and routes it across four scientific divisions; a reviewer audits the evidence and can send the loop back one step before any conclusion is drawn.

  • Chief of Staff — brief, decompose, route
  • 4 divisions — target ID, safety, modality, clinical/literature
  • Scientific Reviewer — gap-gate & re-route
  • CSO synthesis — weighs graded evidence into a call
Layer 2 · tools & validation

Real databases, real validation.

Each division calls deterministic expert tools against live public sources, then validates the signal in silico and against experimental & clinical evidence — never a model guessing from memory.

  • Research: Open Targets · CELLxGENE · TCGA/GDC · GWAS Catalog · openFDA
  • In-silico: celltype-specificity, struct-predictor
  • Experimental: DepMap CRISPR, mouse-KO, trial outcomes
  • Live web: lit-synthesizer (agentic Tavily search)
Layer 3 · verifiability

Every claim is provable.

Results land as cited edges in a persistent knowledge graph; a Vadalog engine derives the verdict deductively with rule-chain explanations; and the whole run is traced span-by-span.

  • Store: canonical KG — kg.py / kg.json
  • Reason: Vadalog / Prometheux — @explain rule chains
  • Trace: trace.jsonl → Langfuse (optional)
  • Grade: provenance + confidence on every fact
