Why it's different

A knowledge graph you can argue with.

Most target tools hand you a score. This one hands you the reasoning — a persistent, canonical evidence graph where entities dedupe across runs and every edge cites its source.

01 — Knowledge

02 — Connections

The loop — runs live, in order

Brief. Route. Audit. Synthesize.

The structure is the product. These four roles run as real agents and stream back as the assessment builds — you watch the case being made.

STEP 01
Chief of Staff

Reads the question, frames the briefing, and decomposes it into what each division must answer.

STEP 02
Division scientists

Four specialists pull graded evidence — expression, specificity, tractability, liability — each cited to source.

STEP 03
Scientific Reviewer

Audits the evidence for gaps and over-reach. If something's missing, it re-routes — one loop back.

STEP 04
CSO synthesis

Weighs the graded evidence into a recommendation, its liabilities, and the uncertainty that remains.

The decision layer

The verdict is deductive, not vibes.

A separate reasoning engine scores the evidence graph against fixed axes and derives the tier. When that engine and the synthesis agent disagree, the dossier says so — out loud — instead of smoothing it over.

  • GO / CONDITIONAL / REVIEW / NO-GO — a named tier with the axes that produced it.
  • Divergence is surfaced — when engine and agent disagree, you see both, not a blend.
  • Explain-a-rank — ask why one target outranks another and read the deciding edges.
